Histories 2.80

1 There is a custom, too, which no Greeks except the Lacedaemonians have in common with the Egyptians: younger men, encountering their elders, yield the way and stand aside, and rise from their seats for them when they approach.

2 But they are like none of the Greeks in this: passers-by do not address each other, but salute by lowering the hand to the knee.
